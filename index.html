Start with a compelling opening paragraph. Make the promise of the article very clear. Readers should understand the topic, the stakes, and the payoff within the first few lines.

Best practice for Medium import Keep the real article content inside this <article> block, use normal headings and paragraphs, and avoid depending on fancy interactive widgets for the core story.

The Problem

Explain the problem in plain language. Use this section to create urgency and context. A strong article usually answers three questions quickly: what is happening, why should the reader care, and what makes your perspective worth reading.

You can also use short lists to improve scannability:

  • State the pain point clearly.
  • Show why simple advice is not enough.
  • Set up the insight that your post will deliver.
“A good article does not just transfer information. It creates momentum from one idea to the next.”

The Solution

Now introduce your main idea. This is where you make the article feel valuable. Use concrete language, clean structure, and examples. When possible, keep paragraphs short. Medium especially rewards readable rhythm.

Make structure visible

Use descriptive subheads instead of generic ones. They help both human readers and skimming editors. If you include code, keep it focused and readable.

Example

Suppose your blog is about building a side project, learning a system, or shipping a technical idea. You can turn raw notes into a publishable article by using a simple arc:

  1. What you noticed
  2. Why it mattered
  3. What you tried
  4. What worked or failed
  5. What others can reuse

That pattern works especially well for engineering, design, AI, productivity, and career posts.

Key Takeaways

  • Lead with a crisp promise.
  • Use semantic HTML and simple structure.
  • Keep the visual design elegant, not overloaded.
  • Make the GitHub Pages version look premium without making Medium import fragile.
